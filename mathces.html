Shortest Chess Match Ever Played
Gibuad (white) Lazard (black)

The chess match between Gibaud and Lazard which to took place at the chess olympiad of 1924, is famous for being extraordinarily brief and decisive. Opening with a standard Pawn to d4 from Gibaud, Lazard responded with tactical sharpness, utilizing his knights effectively. Missteps by Gibaud allowed Lazard to execute a surprising knight fork that exposed critical weaknesses, leading Gibaud to resign quickly. This match remains a classic example of how early tactical awareness and aggressive play can swiftly determine the outcome of a game.
